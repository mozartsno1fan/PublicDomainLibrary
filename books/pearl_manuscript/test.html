SYR GAWAYN AND THE GRENE KNYȜT

FYTTE THE FIRST.

  1. Siþen þe sege & þe assaut watȝ sesed at
  2. Þe borȝ brittened & brent to brondeȝ & askeȝ,
  3. Þe tulk þat þe trammes of tresoun þer wroȝt,
  4. Watȝ tried for his tricherie, þe trewest on erthe;
  5. Hit watȝ Ennias þe athel, & his highe kynde,
  6. Þat siþen depreced prouinces, & patrounes bicome
  7. Welneȝe of al þe wele in þe west iles,
  8. Fro riche Romulus to Rome ricchis hym swyþe,
  9. With gret bobbaunce þat burȝe he biges vpon fyrst,
  10. & neuenes hit his aune nome, as hit now hat;
  11. Ticius to Tuskan [turnes,] & teldes bigynnes;
  12. Langaberde in Lumbardie lyftes vp homes;
  13. & fer ouer þe French flod Felix Brutus
  14. On mony bonkkes ful brode Bretayn he setteȝ,
  15. wyth wynne;
  16. Where werre, & wrake, & wonder,
  17. Bi syþeȝ hatȝ wont þer-inne,
  18. & oft boþe blysse & blunder
  19. Ful skete hathȝ skyfted synne.

  20. Ande quen þis Bretayn watȝ bigged bi þis burn rych;
  21. Bolde bredden þer-inne, baret þat lofden,
  22. In mony turned tyme tene þ at wroȝten;
  23. Mo ferlyes on þis folde han fallen here oft
  24. Þen in any oþer þat I wot, syn þat ilk tyme.
  25. Bot of alle þat here bult of Bretaygne kynges
  26. Ay watȝ Arthur þe hendest; as I haf herde telle;
  27. For-þi an aunter in erde I attle to schawe,
  28. Þat a selly in siȝt summe men hit holden,
  29. & an outtrage awenture of Arthureȝ wondereȝ;
  30. If ȝe wyl lysten þis laye bot on littel quile,
  31. I schal telle hit, as-tit, as I in toun herde,
  32. with tonge;
  33. As hit is stad & stoken,
  34. In stori stif & stronge,
  35. With lel letteres loken,
  36. In londe so hatȝ ben longe.

  37. Þis kyng lay at Camylot vpon kryst-masse,
  38. With mony luflych lorde, ledeȝ of þe best,
  39. Rekenly of þe rounde table alle þo rich breþer,
  40. With rych reuel oryȝt, & rechles merþes;
  41. Þer tournayed tulkes bi-tymeȝ ful mony,
  42. Iusted ful Iolilé þise gentyle kniȝtes,
  43. Syþen kayred to þe court, caroles to make.
  44. For þer þe fest watȝ ilyche ful fiften dayes,
  45. With alle þe mete & þe mirþe þat men couþe a-vyse
  46. Such glaumande gle glorious to here,
  47. Dere dyn vp-on day, daunsyng on nyȝtes,
  48. Al watȝ hap vpon heȝe in halleȝ & chambreȝ,
  49. With lordeȝ & ladies, as leuest him þoȝt;
  50. With all þe wele of þe worlde þay woned þer samen,
  51. Þe most kyd knyȝteȝ vnder kryste seluen,
  52. & þe louelokkest ladies þat euer lif haden,
  53. & he þe comlokest kyng þat þe court haldes;
  54. For al watȝ þis fayre folk in her first age,
  55. on sille;
  56. Þe hapnest vnder heuen,
  57. Kyng hyȝest mon of wylle,
  58. Hit were now gret nye to neuen
  59. So hardy a here on hille.

  60. Wyle nw ȝer watȝ so ȝep þat hit watȝ nwe cummen,
  61. Þat day doubble on þe dece watȝ þe douth serued,
  62. Fro þe kyng watȝ cummen with knyȝtes in to þe halle,
  63. Þe chauntre of þe chapel cheued to an ende;
  64. Loude crye watȝ þe kest of clerkeȝ & oþer,
  65. Nowel nayted o-newe, neuened ful ofte;
  66. & syþen riche forth runnen to reche honde-selle,
  67. Ȝeȝed ȝeres ȝiftes on hiȝ, ȝelde hem bi hond,
  68. Debated busyly about þo giftes;
  69. Ladies laȝed ful loude, þoȝ þay lost haden,
  70. & he þat wan watȝ not wrothe þat may ȝe wel trawe.
  71. Alle þis mirþe þay maden to þe mete tyme;
  72. When þay had waschen, worþyly þay wenten to sete,
  73. Þe best burne ay abof, as hit best semed;
  74. Whene Guenore ful gay, grayþed in þe myddes.
  75. Dressed on þe dere des, dubbed al aboute,
  76. Smal sendal bisides, a selure hir ouer
  77. Of tryed Tolouse, of Tars tapites in-noghe,
  78. Þat were enbrawded & beten wyth þe best gemmes,
  79. Þat myȝt be preued of prys wyth penyes to bye,
  80. in daye;
  81. Þe comlokest to discrye,
  82. Þer glent with yȝen gray,
  83. A semloker þat euer he syȝe,
  84. Soth moȝt no mon say.

  85. Bot Arthure wolde not ete til al were serued,
  86. He watȝ so Ioly of his Ioyfnes, & sum-quat child gered,
  87. His lif liked hym lyȝt, he louied þe lasse
  88. Auþer to lenge lye, or to longe sitte,
  89. So bi-sied him his ȝonge blod & his brayn wylde;
  90. & also anoþer maner meued him eke,
  91. Þat he þurȝ nobelay had nomen, ho wolde neuer ete
  92. Vpon such a dere day, er hym deuised were
  93. Of sum auenturus þyng an vncouþe tale,
  94. Of sum mayn meruayle, þat he myȝt trawe,
  95. Of alderes, of armes, of oþer auenturus,
  96. Oþer sum segg hym bi-soȝt of sum siker knyȝt,
  97. To Ioyne wyth hym in iusyng in Iopardè to lay,
  98. Lade lif for lyf, leve vchon oþer,
  99. As fortune wolde fulsun hom þe fayrer to haue.
  100. Þis watȝ [þe] kynges countenaunce where he in court were,
  101. At vch farand fest among his fre meny,
  102. in halle;
  103. Þer-fore of face so fere.
  104. He stiȝtleȝ stif in stalle,
  105. Ful ȝep in þat nw ȝere,
  106. Much mirthe he was with alle.

  107. Thus þer stondes in stale þe stif kyng his-seluen,
  108. Talkkande bifore þe hyȝe table of trifles ful hende
  109. There gode Gawan watȝ grayþed, Gwenore bisyde
  110. & Agrauayn a la dure mayn on þat oþersyde sittes
  111. Boþe þe kynges sister sunes, & ful siker kniȝtes;
  112. Bischop Bawdewyn abof bi-gineȝ þe table,
  113. & Ywan, Vryn son, ette wit hym-selven;
  114. Þise were diȝt on þe des, & derworþly serued,
  115. & siþen mony siker segge at þe sidbordeȝ.
  116. Þen þe first cors come with crakkyng of trumpes,
  117. Wyth mony baner ful bryȝt, þat þer-bi henged,
  118. Nwe nakryn noyse with þe noble pipes,
  119. Wylde werbles & wyȝt wakned lote,
  120. Þat mony hert ful hiȝe hef at her towches;
  121. Dayntes dryuen þer-wyth of ful dere metes,
  122. Foysoun of þe fresche, & on so fele disches,
  123. Þat pine to fynde þe place þe peple bi-forne
  124. For to sette þe syluener, þat sere sewes halden,
  125. on clothe;
  126. Iche lede as he loued hym-selue
  127. Þer laght with-outen loþe,
  128. Ay two had disches twelue,
  129. Good ber, & bryȝt wyn boþe.

  130. Now wyl I of hor seruise say yow no more,
  131. For vch wyȝe may wel wit no wont þat þer were;
  132. An oþer noyse ful newe neȝed biliue,
  133. Þat þe lude myȝt haf leue lif-lode to cach.
  134. For vneþe watȝ þe noyce not a whyle sesed,
  135. & þe fyrst cource in þe court kyndely serued,
  136. Þer hales in at þe halle dor an aghlich mayster,
  137. On þe most on þe molde on mesure hyghe;
  138. Fro þe swyre to þe swange so sware & so þik,
  139. & his lyndes & his lymes so longe & so grete,
  140. Half etayn in erde I hope þat he were.
  141. Bot mon most I algate mynn hym to bene,
  142. & þat þe myriest in his muckel þat myȝt ride;
  143. For of bak & of brest al were his bodi sturne,
  144. Bot his wombe & his wast were worthily smale,
  145. & alle his fetures folȝande, in forme þat he hade,
  146. ful clene;
  147. For wonder of his hwe men hade,
  148. Set in his semlaunt sene;
  149. He ferde as freke were fade,
  150. & ouer-al enker grene.

  151. Ande al grayþed in grene þis gome & his wedes,
  152. A strayt cote ful streȝt, þat stek on his sides,
  153. A mere mantile abof, mensked with-inne,
  154. With pelure pured apert þe pane ful clene,
  155. With blyþe blaunner ful bryȝt, & his hod boþe,
  156. Þat watȝ laȝt fro his lokkȝ, & layde on his schulderes
  157. Heme wel haled, hose of þat same grene,
  158. Þat spenet on his sparlyr, & clene spures vnder,
  159. Of bryȝt golde, vpon silk bordes, barred ful ryche
  160. & scholes vnder schankes, þere þe schalk rides;
  161. & alle his vesture uerayly watȝ clene verdure,
  162. Boþe þe barres of his belt & oþer blyþe stones,
  163. Þat were richely rayled in his aray clene,
  164. Aboutte hym-self & his sadel, vpon silk werkeȝ,
  165. Þat were to tor for to telle of tryfles þe halue,
  166. Þat were enbrauded abof, wyth bryddes & flyȝes,
  167. With gay gaudi of grene, þe golde ay in myddes;
  168. Þe pendauntes of his payttrure, þe proude cropure
  169. His molaynes, & alle þe metail anamayld was þenne
  170. Þe steropes þat he stod on, stayned of þe same,
  171. & his arsounȝ al after, & his aþel sturtes,
  172. Þat euer glemered & glent al of grene stones.
  173. Þe fole þat he ferkkes on, fun of þat ilke,
  174. sertayn;
  175. A grene hors gret & þikke,
  176. A stede ful stif to strayne,
  177. In brawden brydel quik,
  178. To þe gome he watȝ ful gayn.

  179. Wel gay watȝ þis gome gered in grene,
  180. & þe here of his hed of his hors swete;
  181. Fayre fannand fax vmbe-foldes his schulderes;
  182. A much berd as a busk ouer his brest henges,
  183. Þat myth his hiȝlich here, þat his hed reches,
  184. Watȝ euesed al vmbe-torne, a-bof his elbowes,
  185. Þat half his armes þer vnder were halched in þe wyse
  186. Of a kyngeȝ capados, þat closes his swyre.
  187. Þ e mane of þat mayn bors much to hit lyke,
  188. Wel cresped & cemmed wyth knottes ful mony,
  189. Folden in wyth fildore aboute þe fayre grene.
  190. Ay a herle of þe here, an oþer of golde;
  191. Þe tayl & his toppyng twynnen of a sute,
  192. & bounden boþe wyth a bande of a bryȝt grene,
  193. Dubbed wyth ful dere stoneȝ, as þe dok lasted,
  194. Syþen þrawen wyth a þwong a þwarle knot alofte,
  195. Þer mony belleȝ ful bryȝt of brende golde rungen.
  196. Such a fole vpon folde, ne freke þat hym rydes,
  197. Watȝ neuer sene in þat sale wyth syȝt er þat tyme,
  198. with yȝe;
  199. He loked as layt so lyȝt,
  200. So sayd al þat hym syȝe,
  201. Hit semed as no mon myȝt,
  202. Vnder his dyntteȝ dryȝe.

  203. Wheþer hade he no helme ne hawb[e]rgh nauþer,
  204. Ne no pysan, ne no plate þat pented to armes,
  205. Ne no schafte, ne no schelde, to schwne ne to smyte,
  206. Bot in his on honde he hade a holyn bobbe,
  207. Þat is grattest in grene, when greueȝ ar bare,
  208. & an ax in his oþer, a hoge & vn-mete,
  209. A spetos sparþe to expoun in spelle quo-so myȝt;
  210. Þe hede of an elnȝerde þe large lenkþe hade,
  211. Þe grayn al of grene stele & of golde hewen,
  212. Þe bit burnyst bryȝt, with a brod egge,
  213. As wel schapen to schere as scharp rasores;
  214. Þe stele of a stif staf þe sturne hit bi-grypte,
  215. Þat watȝ wonden wyth yrn to þe wandeȝ ende,
  216. & al bigrauen with grene, in gracios werkes;
  217. A lace lapped aboutem þat louked at þe hede,
  218. & so after þe halme halched ful ofte,
  219. Wuth tryed tasseleȝ þerto tacched in-noghe,
  220. On botounȝ of þe bryȝt grene brayden ful ryche.
  221. Þis haþel heldeȝ hym in, & þe halle entres,
  222. Driuande to þe heȝe dece, dut he no woþe,
  223. Haylsed he neuer one, bot heȝe he ouer loked.
  224. Þe fyrst word þat he warp, « Wher is, » he sayd,
  225. « Þe gouernour of þis gyng? gladly I wolde
  226. « Se þat segg in syȝt, & with hym self speke
  227. raysoun. »
  228. To knyȝteȝ he kest his yȝe
  229. & reled hym vp & doun,
  230. He stemmed & con studie,
  231. Quo walt þer most renoun.

  232. Ther watȝ lokyng on lenþe, þe lude to be-holde,
  233. For vch mon had meruayle quat hit mene myȝt,
  234. Þat a haþel & a horse myȝt such a hwe lach,
  235. As growe grene as þe gres & grener hit semed,
  236. Þ grene aumayl on golde lowande bryȝer,
  237. Al studied þat þer stod, & stalked hym nerre,
  238. Wyth al þe wonder of þe worlde, what he worch schulde.
  239. For fele sellyeȝ had þay sen, bot such neuer are,
  240. For-þi for fantoum & fayryȝe þe fold þere hit demed;
  241. Þer-fore to answare watȝ arȝe mony aþel freke,
  242. & al stouned at his steuen, & stonstil seten,
  243. In a swoghe sylence þurȝ þe sale riche
  244. As al were slypped vpon slepe so slaked hor loteȝ
  245. in hyȝe;
  246. I deme hit not al for doute,
  247. Bot sum for cortaysye,
  248. Bot let hym þat al schulde loute,
  249. Cast vnto þat wyȝe.

  250. Þenn Arþour bifore þe hiȝ dece þat auenture byholdeȝ,
  251. & rekenly hym reuerenced, for rad was he neuer,
  252. & sayde, « Wyȝe, welcum iwys to þis place,
  253. « Þe hede of þis ostel Arthour I hat,
  254. « Liȝt luflych adoun, & lenge, I þe praye,
  255. « & quat so þy wylle is, we schal wyt after. »
  256. « Nay, as help me, » quod þe haþel, « he þat on hyȝe syttes,
  257. « To wone any quyle in þis won, hit watȝ not myn ernde;
  258. « Bot for þe los of þe lede is lyft vp so hyȝe,
  259. « & þy burȝ & þy burnes best ar holden,
  260. « Stifest vnder stel-gere on stedes to ryde,
  261. « Þe wyȝtest & þe worþyest of þe worldes kynde,
  262. « Preue for to play wyth in oþer pure laykeȝ;
  263. « & here is kydde cortaysye, as I haf herd carp,
  264. « & þat hatȝ wayned me hider, I-wyis, at þis tyme.
  265. « Ȝe may be seker bi þis braunch þat I bere here,
  266. « Þat I passe as in pes, & no plyȝt seche;
  267. « For had I founded in fere, in feȝtyng wyse,
  268. « I haue a hauberghe at home & a helme boþe,
  269. « A schelde, & a scharp spere, schinande bryȝt,
  270. « Ande oþer weppenes to welde, I wene wel als,
  271. « Bot for I wolde no were, my wedeȝ ar softer.
  272. « Bot if þou be so bold as alle burneȝ tellen,
  273. « Þou wyl grant me godly þe gomen þat I ask,
  274. bi ryȝt. »
  275. Arthour con onsware,
  276. & sayd, « Sir cortays knyȝt,
  277. « If þou craue batayl bare,
  278. « Here fayleȝ þou not to fyȝt. »

  279. « Nay, frayst I no fyȝt, in fayth I þe telle,
  280. « Hit arn aboute on þis bench bot berdleȝ chylder;
  281. « If I were hasped in armes on a heȝe stede,
  282. « Here is no mon me to mach, for myȝteȝ so wayke.
  283. « For-þy I craue in þis court a crystmas gomen,
  284. « For hit is ȝol & nwe ȝer, & here ar ȝep mony;
  285. « If any so hardy in þis hous holdeȝ hym-seluen,
  286. « Be so bolde in his blod, brayn in hys hede,
  287. « Þat dar stifly strike a strok for an oþer,
  288. « I schal gif hym of my gyft þys giserne ryche,
  289. « Þis ax, þat is heué in-nogh, to hondele as hym lykes,
  290. « & I schal bide þe fyrst bur, as bare as I sitte.
  291. « If any freke be so felle to fonde þat I telle,
  292. « Lepe lyȝtly me to, & lach þis weppen,
  293. « I quit clayme hit for euer, kepe hit as his auen,
  294. « & I achal stonde hym a strok, stif on þis flet,
  295. « Elleȝ þou wyl diȝt me þe dom to dele hym an oþer,
  296. barlayl;
  297. « & ȝet gif hym respite,
  298. « A twelmonyth & a day; —
  299. « Now hyȝe, & let se tite
  300. « Dar any her-inne oȝt say. »

  301. If he hem stowned vpon fyrst, stiller were þanne
  302. Alle þe hered-men in halle, þe hyȝ & þe loȝe;
  303. Þe renk on his rounce hym ruched in his sadel,
  304. & runisch-ly his rede yȝen he reled about,
  305. Bende his bresed broȝeȝ, bly-cande grene,
  306. Wayued his berde for to wayte quo-so wolde ryse.
  307. When non wolde kepe hym with carp he coȝed ful hyȝe,
  308. Ande rimed hym ful richley, & ryȝt hym to speke:
  309. « What, is þis Arþures hous, » quod þe haþel þenne,
  310. « Þat al þe rous rennes of, þurȝ ryalmes so mony?
  311. « Where is now your sourquydrye & your conquestes,
  312. « Your gry[n]del-layk, & your greme, & your grete wordes?
  313. « Now is þe reuel & þe renoun of þe rounde table
  314. « Ouer-walt wyth a worde of on wyȝes speche;
  315. « For al dares for drede, with-oute dynt schewed! »
  316. Wyth þis he laȝes so loude, þat þe lorde greued;
  317. Þe blod schot for scham in-to his schyre face
  318. & lere;
  319. He wex as wroth as wynde,
  320. So did alle þat þer were
  321. Þe kyng as kene bi kynde,
  322. Þen stod þat stif mon nere.

  323. And sayde, « Haþel, by heuen þyn askyng is nys,
  324. « & as þou foly hatȝ frayst, fynde þe be-houes;
  325. « I know no gome þat is gast of þy grete wordes.
  326. « Gif me now þy geserne, vpon godeȝ halue,
  327. « & I schal bayþen þy bone, þat þou boden habbes. »
  328. Lyȝtly lepeȝ he hym to, & laȝt at his honde;
  329. Þen feersly þat oþer freke vpon fote lyȝtis.
  330. Now hatȝ Arthure his axe, & þe halme grypeȝ,
  331. & sturnely stureȝ hit aboute, þat stryke wyth hit þoȝt.
  332. Þe stif mon hym bifore stod vpon hyȝt,
  333. Herre þen ani in þe hous by þe hede & more;
  334. Wyth sturne schere þer he stod, he stroked his berde,
  335. & wyth a countenaunce dryȝe he droȝ doun his cote,
  336. No more mate ne dismayd for hys mayn dinteȝ,
  337. Þen any burne vpon bench hade broȝt hym to drynk
  338. of wyne,
  339. Gawan, þat sate bi þe quene,
  340. To þe kyng he can enclyne,
  341. « I be-seche now with saȝeȝ sene,
  342. « Þis melly mot be myne. »

  343. « Wolde ȝe, worþilych lorde, » quod Gawan to þe kyng,
  344. « Bid me boȝe fro þis bench, & stonde by yow þere,
  345. « Þat I wyth-oute vylanye myȝt voyde þis table,
  346. « & þat my legge lady lyked not ille,
  347. « I wolde come to your counseyl, bifore your court ryche.
  348. « For me þink hit not semly, as hit is soþ knawen,
  349. « Þer such an askyng is heuened so hyȝe in your sale,
  350. « Þaȝȝe ȝour-self be talenttyf to take hit to your-seluen,
  351. « Whil mony so bolde yow aboute vpon bench sytten,
  352. « Þat vnder heuen, I hope, not haȝer er of wylle,
  353. « Ne better bodyes on bent, þer baret is rered;
  354. « I am þe wakkest, I wot, and of wyt feblest,
  355. « & lest lur of my lyf, quo laytes þe soþe,
  356. « Bot for as much as ȝe ar myn em, I am only to prayse,
  357. « No bounté bot your blod I in my bodé knowe;
  358. « & syþen þis note is so nys, þat noȝt hit yow falles,
  359. « & I haue frayned hit at yow fyrst, foldeȝ hit to me,
  360. « & if I carp not comlyly, let alle þis cort ryche,
  361. bout blame. »
  362. Ryche to-geder con roun,
  363. & syþen þe redden alle same,
  364. To ryd þe kyng wyth croun,
  365. & gif Gawan þe game.

  366. Þen comaunded þe kyng þe knyȝt for to ryse;
  367. & he ful radly vp ros, & ruchched hym fayre,
  368. Kneled doun bifore þe kyng, & cacheȝ þat weppen;
  369. & he luflyly hit hym laft, & lyfte vp his honde,
  370. & gef hym goddeȝ blessyng, & gladly hym biddes
  371. Þat his hert & his hond schulde hardi be boþe.
  372. « Kepe þe cosyn, » quod þe kung, « þat þou on kyrf sette,
  373. « & if þou redeȝ hym ryȝt, redly I trowe,
  374. « Þat þhou shal byden þe bur þat he schal bede after. »
  375. Gawan gotȝ to þe gome, with giserne in honde,
  376. & he badly hym bydeȝ, he bayst neuer þe helder
  377. Þen carppeȝ to sir Gawan þe knyȝt in þe grene,
  378. « Refourme we oure for-wardes, er we fyrre passe.
  379. « Fyrst I eþe þe, haþel, how þat þou hattes,
  380. « Þat þou me telle truly, as I tryst may? »
  381. « In god fayth, » quod þe goode knyȝt, « Gawan I hatte,
  382. « Þat bede þe þis buffet, quat-so bi-falleȝ after,
  383. « & at þis tyme twelmonyth take at þe anoþer,
  384. « Wyth what weppen so þou wylt, & wyth no wyȝ elleȝ,
  385. on lyue. »
  386. Þat oþer on-swareȝ agayn,
  387. « Sir Gawan, so mot I þryue,
  388. « As I am ferly fayn.
  389. « Þis dint þat þou schal dryue. »

  390. « Bigog, » quod þe grene knyȝt, « sir Gawan, melykes,
  391. « Þat I schal fange at þy fust þat I haf frayst here;
  392. « & þou hatȝ redily rehersed, bi resoun ful trwe,
  393. « Clanly al þe couenaunt þat I þe kynge asked,
  394. « Saf þat þou schal siker me, segge, bi þi trawþe,
  395. « Þat þou schal seche me þi-self, where-so þou hopes
  396. « I may be funde vpon folde, & foch þe such wages
  397. « As þou deles me to day, bifore þis douþe ryche. »
  398. « Where schulde I wale þe, » quod Gauan, « where is þy place?
  399. « I wot neuer where þou wonyes, bi hym þat me wroȝt,
  400. « Ne I know not þe, knyȝt, þy cort, ne þi name.
  401. « Bot teche me truly þer-to, & telle me howe þou hattes,
  402. « & I schal ware alle my wyt to wynne me þeder,
  403. « & þat I swere þe for soþe, & by my seker traweþ. »
  404. « Þat is in-nogh in nwe ȝer, hit nedes no more, »
  405. Quod þe gome in þe grene to Gawan þe hende,
  406. « Ȝif I þe telle trwly, quen I þe tape haue,
  407. « & þou me smoþely hatȝ smyten, smartly I þe teche
  408. « Of my hous, & my home, & myn owen nome,
  409. « Þen may þou frayst my fare, & forwardeȝ holde,
  410. « & if I spende no speche, þenne spedeȝ þou þe better,
  411. « For þou may leng in þy londe, & layt no fyrre,
  412. bot slokes;
  413. « Ta now þy trymme tole to þe,
  414. « & let se how þou cnokeȝ. »
  415. « Gladly sir, for soþe, »
  416. Quod Gawan; his ax he strokes.

  417. The grene knyȝt vpon grounde grayþely hym dresses,
  418. A littel lut with þe hede, þe lere he discouereȝ,
  419. His longe louelych lokkeȝ he layd ouer his croun.
  420. Let þe naked nec to þe note schewe.
  421. Gauan gripped to his ax, & gederes hit on hyȝt,
  422. Þe kay fot on þe folde he be-fore sette,
  423. Let hit doun lyȝt on þe naked,
  424. Þat þe scharp of þe schalk schyndered þe bones,
  425. & schrank þurȝ þe schyire grece, & scade hit in twynne,
  426. Þat þe bit of þe brown stel bot on þe grounde.
  427. Þe fayre hede fro þe halce hit [felle] to þe erþe,
  428. Þat fele hit foyned wyth her fete, þere hit forth roled;
  429. Þe blod brayd fro þe body, þat blykked on the grene;
  430. & nawþer faltered ne fel þe freke neuer þe helder,
  431. Bot styþly he start forth vpon styf schonkes,
  432. & run[n]yschly he raȝt out, þere as renkkeȝ stoden,
  433. Laȝt to his lufly hed, & lyft hit vp sone;
  434. & syþen boȝeȝ to his blonk, þe brydel he cachcheȝ,
  435. Steppeȝ in to stel bawe & strydeȝ alofte,
  436. & his hede by þe here in his honde haldeȝ;
  437. & as sadly þe segge hym in his sadel sette,
  438. As non vnhap had hym ayled, þaȝ hedleȝ he we[re],
  439. in stedde;
  440. He brayde his bluk aboute,
  441. Þat vgly bodi þat bledde,
  442. Moni on of hym had doute,
  443. Bi þat his resounȝ were redde.

  444. For þe hede in his honde he haldeȝ vp euen,
  445. To-ward þe derrest on þe dece he dresseȝ þe face,
  446. & hit lyfte vp þe yȝe-lyddeȝ, & loked ful brode,
  447. & meled þus much with his uthe, as ȝe may now here.
  448. « Loke, Gawan, þou be grayþe to go as þou hetteȝ,
  449. « & layte as lelly til þou me, lude fynde,
  450. « As þou hatȝ hette in þis halle, herande þise knyȝtes;
  451. « To þe grene chapel þou chose, I charge þe to fotte,
  452. « Such a dunt as þou hatȝ dalt dalt disserued þou habbeȝ,
  453. « To be ȝederly ȝolden on nw ȝeres morn;
  454. « Þe knyȝt of þe grene chapel men knowen me mony;
  455. « For-þis me forto fynde if þou fraysteȝ, fayleȝ þou neuer,
  456. « Þer-fore com, oþer recreaunt be calde þe be-houeus. »
  457. With a runisch rout þe rayneȝ he torneȝ,
  458. Halled out at þe hal-dor, his hed in his hande,
  459. Þat þe fyr of þe flynt flaȝe fro fole houes.
  460. To quat kyth he be-com, knwe non þere,
  461. Neuermore þen þay wyste fram queþen he watȝ wonnen;
  462. what þenne?
  463. Þe kyng & Gawen þare,
  464. At þat grene þay laȝe & grenne,
  465. Ȝet breued watȝ hit ful bare,
  466. A meruayl among þo menne.

  467. Þaȝ Arþer þe hende kyng at hert hade wonder,
  468. He let no semblaunt be sene, bot sayde ful hyȝe
  469. To þe comlych quene, wyth cortays speche,
  470. « Dere dame, to day demay yow neuer;
  471. « Wel by-commes such craft vpon cristmasse,
  472. « Laykyng of enterludeȝ, to laȝe & to syng.
  473. « Among þis, kynde caroles of knyȝteȝ & ladyeȝ;
  474. « Neuer-þe-lece to my mete I may me wel dres,
  475. « For I haf sen a selly, I may not for-sake. »
  476. He glent vpon sir Gawen, & gaynly he sayde,
  477. « Now sir, heng vp þyn ax, þat hatȝ in-nogh hewen. »
  478. & hit watȝ don abof þe dece, on doser to henge,
  479. Þer alle men for meruayl myȝt on hit loke,
  480. & bi trwe tytel þer-of to telle þe wonder.
  481. Þenne þay boȝed to a borde þise burnes to-geder,
  482. Þe kyng & þe gode knyȝt, & kene men hem serued
  483. Of alle dayntyeȝ double, as derrest myȝt falle,
  484. Wyth alle maner of mete & mynstralcie boþe;
  485. Wyth wele walt þay þat day, til worþed an ende,
  486. in londe.
  487. Now þenk wel, sir Gawan,
  488. For woþe þat þou ne wonde,
  489. Þis auenture forto frayn,
  490. Þat þou hatȝ tan on honde.

FYTTE THE SECOND.

  1. This hanselle hatȝ Arthur of auenturus on fyrst,
  2. In ȝonge ȝ, for he ȝerned ȝelpyng to here,
  3. Thaȝ hym wordeȝ were wane, when þay to sete wenten;
  4. Now ar þey stoken of sturne werk staf-ful her hond.
  5. Gawan watȝ glad to be-gynne þose gomneȝ in halle,
  6. Bot þaȝ þe ende be heuy, hafȝe no wonder;
  7. For þaȝ men ben mery in mynde, quen þay han mayn drynk,
  8. A ȝere ȝernes ful ȝerne, & ȝeldeȝ neuer lyke,
  9. Þe forme to þe fynisment foldeȝ ful selden.
  10. For-þi þis ȝol ouer-ȝede, & þe ȝere after,
  11. & vche sesoun serlepes sued after oþer;
  12. After Crysten-masse com þe fyche & crabben lentoun,
  13. Þat fraysteȝ flesch wyth þe fysche & fode more symple
  14. Bot þenne þe weder of þe worlde wyth wynter hit þrepeȝ
  15. Colde clengeȝ adoun, cloudeȝ vp-lyften,
  16. Schyre schedeȝ þe rayn in schowreȝ ful warme,
  17. Falleȝ vpon fayre flat, flowreȝ þere schewen,
  18. Boþe groundeȝ & þe greueȝ grene ar her wedeȝ,
  19. Bryddeȝ busken to bylde, & bremlych syngen,
  20. For solace of þe softe somer þat sues þer after,
  21. bi bonk;
  22. & blossumeȝ bolne to blowe,
  23. Bi raweȝ rych & ronk,
  24. Þen noteȝ noble in-noȝe,
  25. Ar herde in wod so wlonk.

  26. After þe sesoun of somer wyth þe soft wyndeȝ,
  27. Quen ȝeferus sylfeȝ hym-self on sedeȝ & erbeȝ,
  28. Wela-wynne is þe wort þat woxes þer-oute.
  29. When þe donkande dewe dropeȝ of þe leueȝ,
  30. To bide a blysful blusch of þe bryȝt sunne.
  31. Bot þen hyȝes heruest, & herdenes hym sone.
  32. Warneȝ hym for þe wynter to wax ful rype;
  33. He dryues wyth droȝt þe dust for to ryse.
  34. Fro þe face of þe folde to flyȝe ful hyȝe;
  35. Wroþe wynde of þe welkyn wrasteleȝ with þe sunne,
  36. Þe leueȝ lancen fro þe lynde, & lyȝten on þe grounde,
  37. & al grayes þe gres, þat grene watȝ ere;
  38. Þennel al rypeȝ & roteȝþat ros vpon fyrst,
  39. & þus ȝirneȝ þe ȝere in ȝisterdayeȝ mony,
  40. & wynter wyndeȝ aȝayn, as þe worlde askeȝ
  41. no sage.
  42. Til meȝel-mas mone,
  43. Watȝ cumen wyth wynter wage;
  44. Þen þenkkeȝ Gawan ful sone,
  45. Of his anious uyage.

  46. Ȝ quyl al-hal-day with Arþer he lenges,
  47. & he made a fare on þat fest, for þe frekeȝ sake,
  48. With much reuel & ryche of þe rounde table;
  49. Knyȝteȝ ful cortays & comlych ladies,
  50. Al for luf of þat lede in longynge þay were,
  51. Bot neuer-þe-lece ne þe later þay neuened bot merþe,
  52. Mony ioyleȝ for þat ientyle iapeȝ þer maden.
  53. For aftter mete, with mournyng he meleȝ to his eme,
  54. & spekeȝ of his passage, & pertly he sayde,
  55. « Now, lege lorde of my lyf leue I yow ask;
  56. « Ȝe knowe þe cost of þis cace, kepe I no more
  57. « To tell you teneȝ þer-of neuer bot trifel;
  58. « Bot I am boun to þe bur barely to morne,
  59. « To sech þe gome of þe grene, as god wyl me wysse. »
  60. Þenne þe best of þe burȝ boȝed to-geder,
  61. Aywan, & Errik, & oþer ful mony,
  62. Sir Doddinaual de Sauage, þe duk of Clarence,
  63. Launcelot, & Lyonel, & Lucan þe gode,
  64. Sir boos, & sir Byduer, big men boþe,
  65. & mony oþer menskful, with Mador de la Port.
  66. Alle þis compayny of court com þe kyng nerre,
  67. for to counseyl þe knyȝt, with care at her hert;
  68. Þere watȝ much derue doel driuen in þe sale,
  69. Þat so worthe as Wawan schulde wende on þat ernde,
  70. To dryȝe a delful dynt, & dele no more
  71. wyth bronde.
  72. Þe knyȝt mad ay god chere,
  73. & sayde, « Quat schuld I wonde,
  74. « Of destines derf & dere,
  75. « What may mon do bot fonde? »

  76. he dowelleȝ þer al þat day, and dresseȝ on þe morn,
  77. Askeȝ erly hys armeȝ, & alle were þay broȝt
  78. Fyrst a tule tapit, tyȝt ouer þe flet,
  79. & miche watȝ þe gyld gere þat glent þer alofte;
  80. Þe stif mon steppeȝ þeron, & þe stel hondoleȝ,
  81. Dubbed in a dublet of a dere tars,
  82. & syþen a crafty capados, closed aloft,
  83. Þat wyth a bryȝt blaunner was bounden with-inne;
  84. Þenne set þay þe sabatounȝ vpon þe segge foteȝ,
  85. His legeȝ lapped in stel with luflych greueȝ,
  86. With polayneȝ piched þer-to, policed ful clene,
  87. Aboute his kneȝ knaged wyth knoteȝ of golde;
  88. Queme quyssewes þen, þat coyntlych closed
  89. His think þrawen þyȝeȝ with þwonges to-tachched;
  90. & syþen þe brawden bryne of bryȝt stel ryngeȝ,
  91. Vmbe-weued þat wyȝ, vpon wlonk stuffe;
  92. & wel bornyst brace vpon his boþe armes,
  93. With gode cowters & gay, & gloueȝ of plate,
  94. & alle þe godlych gere þat hym gayn schulde
  95. Þat tyde;
  96. Wyth ryche cote armure,
  97. His gold sporeȝ with pryde,
  98. Gurde wyth a bront ful sure,
  99. With silk sayn vmbe his syde.

  100. When he watȝ hasped in armes, his harnays watȝ ryche,
  101. Þe lest lachet ou[þ]er loupe lemed of golde;
  102. So harnayst as he watȝ he herkneȝ in masse,
  103. Offred & honoured at þe heȝe auter;
  104. Syþen he comeȝ to þe kyng & to his cort fereȝ,
  105. Lacheȝ lufly his leue at lordeȝ & ladyeȝ
  106. & þay hym kyst & conueyed, bikende hym to kryst.
  107. Bi þat whatȝ Gryngolet grayth, & gurde with a sadel,
  108. Þat glemed ful gayly with mony golde frenges,
  109. Ay quere naylet ful nwe for þat note ryched;
  110. Þe brydel barred aboute, with bryȝt golde bounded;
  111. Þe apparayl of þe payttrure, & of þe proude skyrteȝ,
  112. Þe cropore, & þe couertor, acorded wyth þe arsouneȝ;
  113. & al watȝ rayled on red ryche golde nayleȝ,
  114. Þat al glytered & glent as glem of þe sunne.
  115. Þenne hentes he þe holme, & hastily hit kysses,
  116. Þat watȝ stapled stifly, & stoffed wyth-inne:
  117. Hit watȝ hyȝe on his hede, hasped bihynde,
  118. Wyth a lyȝtli vrysoun ouer þe auentayle,
  119. Enbrawden & bounded wyth þe best gemmeȝ,
  120. On brode sylkyn borde, & bryddeȝ on semeȝ,
  121. As papiayeȝ paynted pernyng bitwene,
  122. Tortors & trulofeȝ entayled so þyk,
  123. As mony burde þer aboute had ben seuen wynter
  124. in toune;
  125. Þe cercle watȝ more o prys,
  126. Þat vmbe-clypped hys croun,
  127. Of diamaunteȝ a deuys,
  128. Þat boþe were bryȝt & broun.

  129. Then þay schwewd hym þe schelde, þat was of schyr gouleȝ,
  130. Wyth þe pentangel de-paynt of pure golde hweȝ;
  131. He braydeȝ hit by þe baude-ryk, aboute þe hals kestes,
  132. Þat bisemed þe segge semlyly fayre.
  133. & quy þe pentangle apendeȝ to þat prynce noble,
  134. I am in tent yow to telle, þof tary hyt me schulde;
  135. Hit is a syngne þat Salamon set sum-quyle,
  136. In bytoknyng of trawþe, bi tytle þat hit habbeȝ,
  137. For hit is a figure þat heldeȝ fyue poynteȝ,
  138. & vche lyne vmbe-lappeȝ & loukeȝ in oþer,
  139. & ay quere hit is endeleȝ, & Englych hit callen
  140. Ouer-al, as I here, þe endeles knot.
  141. For-þy hit acordeȝ to þis knyȝt, & to his cler armeȝ,
  142. For ay faythful in fyue & sere fyue syþeȝ,
  143. Gawan watȝ for gode knawen, & as golde pured,
  144. Voyded of vche vylany, wyth vertueȝ ennourned
  145. in mote;
  146. For-þy þe pen-tangel nwe
  147. Her ber in schelde & cote,
  148. As tulk of tale most trwe,
  149. & gentylest knyȝt of lote.

  150. Fyrst he watȝ funden fauleȝ in his fyue wytteȝ,
  151. & efte fayled neuer þe freke in his fyue fyngres,
  152. & alle his afyaunce vpon folde watȝ in þe fyue woundeȝ
  153. Þat Cryst kaȝt on þe croys, as þe crede telleȝ
  154. & quere-so-euer þys mon in melly watȝ stad,
  155. His þro þoȝt watȝ in þat, þurȝ alle oþer þyngeȝ,
  156. Þat all his forsnes he fong at þe fyve ioyeȝ,
  157. Þat þe hende heven quene had of hir chylde;
  158. At þis cause þe knyȝt comlyche hade
  159. In þe more half of his schelde hir ymage depaynted,
  160. Þat quen he blusched þerto, his belde neuer payred.
  161. Þe fyrst fyue þat I finde þat þe frek vsed,
  162. Watȝ fraunchyse, & felaȝschyp for-be al þyng;
  163. His clannes & his cortaysye croked were neuer,
  164. & pite, þat passeȝ all poynteȝ, þyse pure fyue
  165. Where harder happed on þat haþel þen on any oþer.
  166. Now alle þese fyve syþeȝ, forsoþe, were fetled on þis knyȝt,
  167. & vchone halched in oþer, þat non ende hade,
  168. & fyched vpon fyue poynteȝ, þat fayld neuer,
  169. Ne samned neuer in no syde, ne sundred nouþ[er],
  170. With-outen ende at any noke [a]i quere fynde,
  171. Where-euer þe gomen bygan, or gold to an ende.
  172. Þer-fore on his schene schelde schapen watȝ þe knot,
  173. Þus alle wyth red golde vpon rede gowleȝ,
  174. Þat is þe pure pentaungel wyth þe peple called,
  175. with lore.
  176. Now grayþed is Gawan gay,
  177. & laȝt his launce ryȝt þore,
  178. & gef hem alle goud day,
  179. He wende for euer more.

  180. H sperred þe sted with þe spureȝ, & sprong on his way,
  181. So stif þat þe ston fyr stroke out þer-after;
  182. Al þat seȝ þat semly suked in hert,
  183. & sayde soþly al same segges til oþer,
  184. Carande for þat comly, « Bi Kryst, hit is scaþe,
  185. « Þat þou, leude, schal be lost, þat art of lyf noble!
  186. « To fynde hys fere vpon folde, in fayth is not eþe;
  187. « Warloker to haf wroȝt had more wyt bene,
  188. « & haf dyȝt ȝonder dere duk to haue worþed;
  189. « A lowande leder of ledeȝ in londe hym wel semeȝ,
  190. « & so had better haf ben þen britned to noȝt,

  529. & þay two tented þayres.

  530. Much dut watȝ þer dryuen þat day & þat oþer,
  531. & þe þryd as þro þronge in þerafter;
  532. Þe ioye of sayn Ioneȝ day watȝ gentyle to here,
  533. & wer gestes to go vpon þe layk, leudeȝ þer þoȝten.
  534. Þer were gestes to go vpon þe gray morne,
  535. For-þy wonderly þay woke, & þe wyn dronken,
  536. Daunsed ful dreȝly wyth dere caroleȝ;
  537. At þe last, when hit watȝ late, þay lachen her leuen,
  538. Vchon to wende on his way, þat watȝ wyȝe stronge.
  539. Gawan gef hym god-day, þe god mon hym lachcheȝ,
  540. Ledes hym to his awen chambre, þ[e] chymné bysyde,
  541. & þere he draȝeȝ hym on dryȝe, & derely hym þonkkeȝ,
  542. Of þe wynne worschip & he hym sayned hade,
  543. As to honour his hous on þat hyȝe tyde,
  544. & enbelyse his burȝ with his bele chere.
  545. « I-wysse sir, quyl I leue, my worþeȝ þe better,
  546. « Þat Gawayn hatȝ ben my gest, at Goddeȝ awen fest. »
  547. « Grant merci sir, » quod Gawayn, « in god fayth hit is yowereȝ,
  548. « Al þe honour is your awen, þe heȝe kyng yow ȝelde;
  549. « & I am wyȝe at your wylle, to worch youre hest,
  550. « As I am halden þer-to, in hyȝe & in loȝe,
  551. bi riȝt. »
  552. Þe lorde fast can bym payne,
  553. To holde lenger þe knyȝt,
  554. To hym answreȝ Gawayn,
  555. Bi non way þat he myȝt.

  556. Then frayned þe freke ful fayre at him-seluen,
  557. Quant derne dede had hym drynken, at þat dere tyme,
  558. So kenly fro þe kyngeȝ kourt to kayre al his one,
  559. Er þe halidayeȝ holly were halet out of toun?
  560. « For soþe sir, » quod þe segge, « ȝe sayn bot þe trawþe
  561. « A haȝe ernde & a hastly me hade fro þo woneȝ,
  562. « For I am sumned my selfe to sech to a place,
  563. « I wot in worlde wheder warde to wende, hit to fynde;
  564. « I nolde, bot if I hit negh myȝt on nwȝeres morne,
  565. « For alle þe londe in-wyth Logres, so me oure lorde hlep!
  566. « For-þy, sir, þis enquest I require yow here,
  567. « Þat ȝe me telle with trawþe, if euer ȝe tale herde
  568. « Of þe grene chapel, quere hit on grounde stondeȝ,
  569. « & of þe knyȝt þat mon at þat mere, of colour of grene?
  570. « « Þer watȝ stabled bi statut a steuen vus by-twene,
  571. « To mete þat mon at þat mere, ȝif I myȝt last;
  572. « & of þat ilk nwȝere hot neked now wonteȝ,
  573. « & I wolde loke on þat lede, if God me let wolde,
  574. « Gladloker, bi Goddeȝ sun, þen any god welde!
  575. « For-þi, I-wysse, bi ȝowre wylle, wende me bi-houes,
  576. « Naf I now to busy bot bare þre dayeȝ,
  577. « & me als fayn to falle feye as fayly of myyn ernde. »
  578. Þenne laȝande quod þe lorde, « Now leng þe by-houes,
  579. « For I schal teche yow to þa[t] terme bi þe tymeȝ ende,
  580. « Þe grene chapayle vpon grounde, greue yow no more;
  581. « Bot ȝe schal be in yowre bed, burne, at þyn ese,
  582. « Quyle forth dayej, & ferk on pe fyrst of pe ȝere,
  583. « & cum to þat merk at mydmorn, to make quat yow likeȝ
  584. in spenne;
  585. « Dowelleȝ whyle new ȝeres daye,
  586. « & rys, & raykeȝ þenne,
  587. « Mon schal yow sette in waye,
  588. « Hit is not two myle henne. »

  589. Þenne watȝ Gawan ful glad, & gomenly he laȝed, —
  590. « Now I þonk yow þryuandely þurȝ alle oþer þynge,
  591. « Now acheued is my chaunce, I schal at your wylle
  592. « Dowelle, & elleȝ do quat ȝe demen. »
  593. Þenne sesed hym þe syre, & set hym bysyde,
  594. Let þe ladieȝ be fette, to lyke hem þe better;
  595. Þer watȝ seme solace by hem-self stille;
  596. Þe lorde let for luf loteȝ so myry,
  597. As wyȝ þat wolde of his wyte, ne wyst quat he myȝt.
  598. Þenne he carped to þe knyȝt, crainde loude,
  599. « Ȝe han demed to do þe debe þat I bidde;
  600. « Wyl ȝe halde þis hes here at þys oneȝ? »
  601. « Ȝe sir, for-soþe, » sayd þe segge trwe,
  602. « Whyl I byde in yowre borȝe, be bayn to ȝow[r]e hest. »
  603. « For ȝe haf trauayled, » quod þe tulk, « towen fro ferre,
  604. « & syþen waked me wyth, þe arn not wel waryst,
  605. « Nauþer of sostnaunce ne of slepe, soþly I knowe;
  606. « Ȝe schal lenge in your lofte, & lyȝe in your ese,
  607. « To morn quyle þe messe-quyle, & to mete wende,
  608. « When ȝe wyl, wyth my wyf, þat wyth yow schal sitte,
  609. « & comfort yow with compayny, til I to cort torne,
  610. ȝe lende;
  611. « & I schal erly ryse,
  612. « On huntyng wyl I wende. »
  613. Gauayn granteȝ alle þyse,
  614. Hym heldande, as þe hende.

  615. « Ȝet firre, » quod þe freke, « a forwarde we make;
  616. « Quat-so-euer I wynne in þe wod, hit worþeȝ to youreȝ,
  617. « & quat chek so ȝe acheue, chaunge me þer-forne;
  618. « Swete, swap we so, sware with trawþe,
  619. « Queþer, leude, so lymp lere oþer better. »
  620. « Bi God, » quod Gawayn þe gode, « I grant þer-tylle,
  621. « & þat yow lyst forto layke, lef hit me þynkes. »
  622. « Who bringeȝ vus þis bargayn is maked: »
  623. So sayde þe lorde of þat lede; þay laȝed vchone,
  624. Þay dronken, & daylyeden, & dalten vntyȝtel,
  625. Þise lordeȝ & ladyeȝ, quyle þat hem lyked;
  626. & syþen with frenkysch fare & fele fayre loteȝ
  627. Þay stoden, & stemed, & stylly speken,
  628. Kysten ful comlyly, & kaȝten her leue.
  629. With mony leude ful lyȝt, & lemande torches,
  630. Vche burne to his bed watȝ broȝt at þe laste,
  631. ful softe;
  632. To bed ȝet er þay ȝede,
  633. Recorded couenaunteȝ oft;
  634. Þe olde lorde of þat leude,
  635. Cowþe wel halde layk a-lofte.

FYTTE THE THIRD.

  1. Ful erly bifore þe day þe folk vp-rysen,
  2. Gestes þat go wolde, hor gromeȝ þay calde,
  3. & þay busken vp bilyue, blonkkeȝ to sadel,
  4. Tyffen he[r] takles, trussen her males,
  5. Richen hem þe rychest, to ryde alle arayde,
  6. Lepen vp lyȝtly, lachen her brydeles,
  7. Vche wyȝe on his way, þere hym wel lyked.
  8. Þe leue lorde of þe londe watȝ not þe last,
  9. A-rayed for þe rydyng, with renkkeȝ ful mony;
  10. Ete a sop hastyly, when he hade herde masse,
  11. With bugle to bent felde he buskeȝ by-lyue;
  12. By þat þat any day-lyȝt lerned vpon erþe,
  13. He with his haþeles on hyȝe horsses weren.
  14. Þenne þise cacheres þat couþe, cowpled hor houndeȝ,
  15. Vnclosed þe kenel dore, & calde hem þer-oute,
  16. Blwe bygly in bugleȝ þre bare mote;
  17. Braches bayed þerefore, & breme noyse maked,
  18. & þay chastysed, & charred, on chasyng þat went;
  19. A hundreth of hunteres, as I haf herde telle,
  20. of þe best;
  21. To trystors vewters ȝod,
  22. Couples huntes of kest,
  23. Þer ros for blasteȝ gode,
  24. Get rurd in þat forest.

  25. At þe fyrst quethe of þe quest quaked þe wylde;
  26. Der drof in þe dale, doted for drede,
  27. Hiȝed to þe hyȝe, bot heterly þay were
  28. Restayed with þe stablye, þat stoutly ascryed;
  29. Þay let þe hertteȝ haf þe gate, with þe hyȝe hedes,
  30. Þe breme bukkeȝ also, with hor brode paumeȝ;
  31. For þe fre lorde hade de-fende in fermysoun tyme,
  32. Þat þer schulde no mon mene to þe male dere.
  33. Þe hindeȝ were halden in, with hay & war,
  34. Þe does dryuen with gret dyn to þe depe sladeȝ;
  35. Þer myȝt mon se, as þay slypte, slentyng of arwes,
  36. At vche [þat] wende vnder wande wapped a flone,
  37. Þat bigly bote on þe broun, with ful brode hedeȝ,
  38. What! þay brayen, & bleden, bi bonkkeȝ þay deȝen.
  39. & ay rachches in a res radly hem folȝes,
  40. Huntereȝ wyth hyȝe horne hasted hem after,
  41. Wyth such a crakkande kry, as klyffes haden brusten;
  42. What wylde so at-waped wyȝes þat schotten,
  43. Watȝ al to-raced & rent, at þe resayt.
  44. Bi þay were tened at þe hyȝe, & taysed to þe wattreȝ,
  45. Þe ledeȝ were so lerned at þe loȝe trysteres,
  46. & þe gre-houndeȝ so grete, þat geten hem bylyue,
  47. & hem to fylched, as fast as frekeȝ myȝt loke,
  48. þer ryȝt.
  49. Þe lorde of blys abloy
  50. Ful oft con launce & lyȝt,
  51. & drof þat day wyth Ioy
  52. Thus to þe derk nyȝt.

  53. Þus laykeȝ þis lorde by lynde wodeȝ eueȝ,
  54. & G. þe god mon, in gay bed laygȝ,
  55. Lurkkeȝ quyl þe day-lyȝt lemed on þe wowes,
  56. Vnder couertour ful clere, cortyned aboute;
  57. & as in slomeryng he slode, sleȝly he herde
  58. A little dyn at his dor, & derfly vpon;
  59. & he heueȝ vp his hed out of þe cloþes,
  60. A corner of þe cortyn he caȝt vp a lyttel,
  61. & wayteȝ warly þider-warde, quat hit be myȝt.
  62. Hit watȝ þe ladi, loflyest to be-holde,
  63. Þat droȝ þe dor after hir ful dernly & stylle,
  64. & boȝed to-warde þe bed; & þe burne schamed.
  65. & layde hym doun lystyly, & let as he slepte.
  66. & ho stepped stilly. & stel to his bedde,
  67. Kest vp þe cortyn, & creped with-inne,
  68. & set hir ful softly on þe bed-syde,
  69. & lenged þere selly longe, to loke quen he wakened.
  70. Þe lede lay lurked a ful longe quyle,
  71. Compast in his concience to quat þat cace myȝt
  72. Mene oþer amount, to meruayle hym þoȝt;
  73. Bot ȝet he sayde in hym-self, « More semly hit were
  74. « To aspye wuth my spelle [in] space quat ho wolde.»
  75. Þen he wakenede, & wroth, & to hir warde torned,
  76. & vn-louked his yȝe-lyddeȝ, & let as hym wondered,
  77. & sayned hym, as bi his saȝe þe sauer to worthe,
  78. with hande;
  79. Wyth chynne & cheke ful swete,
  80. Boþe quit & red in-lbande,
  81. Ful lufly con ho lete,
  82. Wyth lyppeȝ smal laȝande.

  83. « God moroun, sir Gawayn, » sayde þat fayr lady,
  84. « Ȝe ar a sleper vn-slyȝe, þat mon may slyde hider;
  85. « Now ar ȝe tan astyt, bot true vus may schape,
  86. « I schal bynde yow in your bedde, þat be ȝe trayst. »
  87. Al laȝande þe lady lanced þo bourdeȝ.
  88. « Goud moroun g[aye], » quod Gawayn þe blyþe.
  89. « Me schal worþe at your wille, & þat me wel lykeȝ,
  90. « For I ȝelde me ȝederly, & ȝeȝe after grace,
  91. « & þat is þe best, be my dome, for me by-houeȝ nede; »
  92. & þus he bourded a-ȝayn with mony a blyþe laȝter.
  93. « Bot wolde ȝe, lady louely, þen leue me grante,
  94. « & de-prece your prysoun, & pry hym to ryse,
  95. « I wolde boȝe of þis bed, & busk me better,
  96. « I schulde keuer þe more comfort to karp yow wyth. »
  97. « Nay, for soþe, beau sir, » sayd þat swete,
  98. « Ȝe schal not rise of your bedde, I rych yow better,
  99. « I schal happe yow here þat oþer half als,
  100. « & syþen karp wyth my knyȝt þat I kaȝt haue;
  101. « For I wene wel, Iwysse, sir Wawen ȝe are,
  102. « Þat alle þe worlde worchipeȝ, quere-so ȝe ride;
  103. « Your honour, your hendelayk is hendely praysed
  104. « With lordeȝ, wyth ladyes, with alle þat lyf bere.
  105. « & now ȝe ar here, iwysse, & we bot oure one;
  106. « My lorde & his ledeȝ ar on lenþe faren,
  107. « Oþer burneȝ in her bedde, & my burdeȝ als,
  108. « Þe dor drawen, & dit with a derf haspe;
  109. « & syþen I haue in þis hous hym þat al lykeȝ,
  110. « I schal ware my whyle wel, quyl hit lasteȝ,
  111. with tale;
  112. « Ȝe ar welcum to my cors,
  113. « Yowre awen won to wale,
  114. « Me be-houeȝ of fyne force,
  115. « Your seruaunt be & schale. »

  116. « In god fayth, » quod Gawayn, « gayn hit me þynkkeȝ,
  117. « Þaȝ I be not now he þat ȝe of speken;
  118. « To reche to such reuerence as ȝe reherce here
  119. « I am wyȝe vn-worþy, I wot wel my-seluen;
  120. « Bi God, I were glad, & yow god þoȝt,
  121. « At saȝe oþer at seruyce þat I sette myȝt
  122. « To þe plesaunce of your prys, hit were a pure ioye. »
  123. « In god Fayth, sir Gawayn, » quod þe gay lady,
  124. « Þe prys & þe prowes þat pleseȝ al oþer,
  125. « If I hit lakked, oþer set at lyȝt, hit were littel daynté;
  126. « Bot hit ar ladyes in-noȝe, þat leuer wer nowþe,
  127. « Haf þe hende in hor holde, as I þe habbe here,
  128. « To daly witt derely your daynté wordeȝ,
  129. « Keuer hem comfort, & colen her careȝ,
  130. « Þen much of þe garysourn oþer golde þat þay hauen;
  131. « Bot I louue þat ilk lorde þat þe lyfte haldeȝ,
  132. « I haf hit holly in my honde þat al desyres,
  133. þurȝe grace. »
  134. Scho made hym so gret chere,
  135. Þat watȝ so fayr of face,
  136. Þe knyȝt with speches skere,
  137. A[n]swared to vche a cace.

  138. « Madame, » quod þe myry mon, « Mary yow ȝelde,
  139. « For I haf founden, in god fayth, yowre fraunchis nobele,
  140. « & oþer ful much of oþer folk fongen hor dedeȝ;
  141. « Bot þe daynté þat þay delen for my disert nysen,
  142. « Hit is þe worchyp of your-self, þat noȝt hot wel conneȝ. »
  143. « Bi Mary, » quod þe menskful, « me þynk hit anoþer;
  144. « For were I worth al þe wone of mymmen alyue,
  145. « & al þe wele of þe worlde were in my honde,
  146. « & I schulde chepen & chose, to cheue me a lorde,
  147. « For þe costes þat I haf knowen vpun þe knyȝt here,
  148. « Of bewté, & debonerté, & blyþe semblaunt,
  149. « & þat I haf er herkkened, & halde hit here trwee,
  150. « Þer schulde no freke vpon folde bifore yow be chosen. »
  151. « I-wysse, worþy, » quod þe wyȝe, « ȝe haf waled wel better,
  152. « Bot I am proude of þe prys þat ȝe put on me,
  153. « & soberly your seruaunt my souerayn I holde yow,
  154. « & yowre knyȝt I be-com, & Kryst yow for-ȝelde. »
  155. Þus þay meled of much-quat, til myd-morn paste,
  156. & ay þe lady let lyk, a hym loued mych;
  157. Þe freke ferde with defence, & feted ful fayre.
  158. Þaȝ I were burde bryȝtest, þe burde in mynde hade,
  159. Þe lasse luf in his lode, for lur þat he soȝt,
  160. boute hone;
  161. Þe dunte þat schulde hym deue,
  162. & nedeȝ hit most be done;
  163. Þe lady þenn spek of leue.
  164. He granted hir ful sone.

  165. Þenne ho gef hym god-day, & wyth a glent laȝed.
  166. & as ho stod, ho stonyed hym wyth ful stor wordeȝ:
  167. « Now he þat spedeȝ vche spech, þis disport ȝelde yow!
  168. « Bot þat ȝe be Gawan, hit gotȝ in mynde. »
  169. « Qyer-fore? » quod þe freke, & freschly he askeȝ,
  170. Ferde lest he hade fayled in fourme of his castes;
  171. Bot þe burde hym blessed, & bi þis skyl sayde,
  172. « So god as Gawayn gaynly is halden,
  173. « & cortaysye is closed so clene in hym-seluen,
  174. « Couth not lyȝtly haf lenged so long wyth a lady,
  175. « Bot he craued a cosse, bi his courtaysye,
  176. « Bi sum towch of summe tryfle, at sum taleȝ ende. »
  177. Þen quod Wowen, « I-wysse, worþe as yow lykeȝ,
  178. « I schal kysse at your comaundement, as a knyȝt falleȝ,
  179. « & fire lest he displese yow, so plede hit no more. »
  180. Ho comes nerre with þat, & cacheȝ hym in armeȝ,
  181. Louteȝ luflych adoun, & þe leude kysseȝ;
  182. Þay comly bykennen to Kryst ayþer oþer;
  183. Ho dos hir forth at þe dore, with-outen dyn more.
  184. & he ryches hym to ryse, & rapes hym sone,
  185. Clepes to his chamberlayn, choses his wede,
  186. Boȝeȝ forth, quen he watȝ boun, blyþely to masse,
  187. & þenne he meued to his mete, þat menskly hym keped,
  188. & made myry al day til þe mone rysed,
  189. with game;
  190. With neuer freke fayrer fonge,
  191. Bitwene two so dyngne dame,
  192. Þe alder & þe ȝonge,
  193. Much solace set þay same.

  194. And ay þe lorde of þe londe is lent on his gamneȝ,
  195. To hunt in holteȝ & heþe, at hyndeȝ barayne,
  196. Such a sowme he þer slowe bi þat þe sunne heldet,
  197. Of dos & of oþer dere, to deme were wonder.
  198. Þenne fersly þay flokked in folk at þe laste,
  199. & quykly of þe quelled dere a querré þay maked;
  200. Þe best boȝed þerto, with burneȝ in-noghe,
  201. Gedered þe grattest of gres þat þer were,
  202. & didden hem derely vndo, as þe dede askeȝ;
  203. Serched hem at þe asay, summe þat þer were,
  204. Two fyngeres þay fonde of þe fowlest of alle;
  205. Syþen þay slyt þe slot, sesed þe erber,
  206. Schaued wyth a scharp knyf, & þe schyre knitten;
  207. Syþen rytte þay þe foure lymmes, & rent of þe hyde,
  208. Þen brek þay þe bale, þe baleȝ out token,
  209. Lystily forlancyng, & bere of þe knot;
  210. Þay gryped to þe gargulun, & grayþely departed
  211. Þe wesaunt fro þewynt-hole, & walt out þe gutteȝ;
  212. Þen scher þay out þe schuldereȝ with her scharp knyueȝ,
  213. Haled hem by a lyttel hole, to haue hole sydes;
  214. Siþen britned þay þe brest, & braydenhit in twynne,
  215. & eft at þe gargulun bigyneȝ on þenne,
  216. Ryueȝ hit vp radly, ryȝt to þe byȝt,
  217. Voydeȝ out þe a-vanters, & verayly þerafter
  218. Alle þ rymeȝ by þe rybbeȝ radly þay lance;

  544. To norne on þe same note, on nweȝereȝ euen;
  545. Bot þe knyȝt craued leue, to kayre on þe morn,
  546. for hit watȝ neȝ at þe terme, þat he to schulde.
  547. Þe lorde hym letted of þat, to lenge hym resteyed,
  548. & sayde, « As I am trwe segge, I siker my trawþe,
  549. « Þou schal cheue to þe grene chapel, þy charres to make,
  550. « Leude, on nwȝereȝ lyȝt, long bifore pryme:
  551. « For-þy þow lye in þy loft, & lach þyn ese,
  552. « & I schal hunt in þis holt, & halde þe towcheȝ,
  553. « Chaunge wyth þe cheuisaunce, bi þat I charre hider;
  554. « For I haf fraysted þe twys, & faythful I fynde þe,
  555. « Now þrid tyme þrowe best þenk on þe morne,
  556. « Make we mery quyl we may, & mynne vpon Ioye,
  557. « For þe lur may mon lach, when so mon lykeȝ. »
  558. Þis watȝ grayþely graunted, & Gawayn is lenged,
  559. Bliþe broȝt watȝ hym drynk, & þay to bedde ȝeden,
  560. with liȝt;
  561. Sir G: lis & slepes,
  562. Ful stille & softe al niȝt;
  563. Þe lorde þat his crafteȝ kepes,
  564. Ful erly he watȝ diȝt.

  565. After messe a morsel he & his men token,
  566. Miry watȝ þe mornyng, his mounture he askes;
  567. Alle þe haþeles þat on horse schulde helden hym after,
  568. Were boun busked on hor blonkkeȝ, bi-fore þe halle ȝateȝ;
  569. Ferly fayre watȝ þe folde, for þe forst clenged,
  570. In rede rudede vpon rak rises þe sunne,
  571. & ful clere costeȝ þe clowdes of þe welkyn.
  572. Hunteres vnhardeled bi a holt syde,
  573. Rocheres roungen bi rys, for rurde of her hornes;
  574. Summe fel in þe fute, þer þe fox bade,
  575. Trayleȝ ofte a trayteres, bi traunt of her wyles;
  576. A kenet kryes þerof, þe hunt on hym calles,
  577. His felaȝes fallen hym to, þat fnasted ful þike,
  578. Runnen forth in a rabel, in his ryȝt fare;
  579. & he fyskeȝ hem by-fore, þay founden hym sone,
  580. & quen þay seghe hym with syȝt, þay sued hym fast,
  581. Wreȝande h[ym] ful [w]eterly with a wroth noyse;
  582. & he trantes & tornayeeȝ þurȝ mony tene greue;
  583. Hamlouneȝ, & herkeneȝ, bi heggeȝ ful ofte;
  584. At þe last bi a littel dich he lepeȝ ouer a spenné,
  585. Steleȝ out ful stilly bi a strothe rande,
  586. Went haf wylt of þe wode, with wyleȝ fro þe houndes,
  587. Þenne watȝ he went, er he wyst, to a wale tryster,
  588. Þer þre þro at a þrich þrat hym at ones,
  589. al graye;
  590. He blenched aȝayn bilyue,
  591. & stifly start onstray,
  592. With alle þe wo on lyue,
  593. To þe wod he went away.

  594. Thenne watȝ hit lif vpon list to lyþen þe houndeȝ,
  595. When alle þe mute hade hym met, menged to-geder,
  596. Suche a sorȝe at þat syȝt þay sette on his hede,
  597. As alle þe clamberande clyffes hade clatered on hepes;
  598. Here he watȝ halawed, when haþeleȝ hym metten,
  599. Loude he watȝ ȝayned, with ȝarande speche;
  600. Þer he watȝ þreted, & ofte þef called,
  601. & ay þe titleres at his tayl, þat tary he ne myȝt;
  602. Ofte he watȝ runnen at, when he out rayked,
  603. & ofte reled in aȝayn, so reniarde watȝ wylé.
  604. & ȝe he lad hem bi lig, mon, þe lorde & his meyny;
  605. On þis maner bi þe mountes, quyle myd, ouer, vnder,
  606. Whyle þe hende knyȝt at home holsumly slepeȝ,
  607. With-inne þe comly cortynes, on þe colde morne.
  608. Bot þe lady for luf let not to slepe,
  609. Ne þe purpose to payre, þat pyȝt in hir hert,
  610. Bot ros hir vp radly, rayked hir þeder,
  611. In a mery mantyle, mete to þe erþe,
  612. Þat watȝ furred ful fyne with felleȝ, wel pured,
  613. No hweȝ goud on hir hede, bot þe haȝer stones
  614. Trased aboute hir tressour, be twenty in clusteres;
  615. Hir þryuen face & hir þrote þrowen al naked,
  616. Hir brest bare bifore, & bihinde eke.
  617. Ho comeȝ with-inne þe chambre dore, & closes hit hir after,
  618. Wayneȝ vp a wyndow, & on þe wyȝe calleȝ,
  619. & radly þus re-hayted hym, with hir riche wordeȝ,
  620. with chere;
  621. « A! mon, how may þou slepe,
  622. « Þis morning is so clere? »
  623. He watȝ in drowping depe,
  624. Bot þenne he con hir here.

  625. In dreȝ droupyng of dreme draueled þat noble,
  626. As mon þat watȝ in mornyng of mony þro þoȝtes,
  627. How þat destiné schulde þat day [dyȝt] his wyrde,
  628. At þe grene chapel, when he þe gome metes,
  629. & hi-houes his buffet abide, with-oute debate more;
  630. Bot quen þat com;y he keuered his wyttes,
  631. Swenges out of þe sweuenes, & swareȝ with hast.
  632. Þe lady luflych com laȝande swete,
  633. Felle ouer his fayre face, & fetly him kyssed;
  634. He welcumeȝ hir worþily, with a wale chere;
  635. He seȝ hir so glorious, & gayly atyred,
  636. So fautles of hir fetures, & of so fyne hewes,
  637. Wiȝt wallande Ioye warmed his hert;
  638. With smoþe smylyng & smolt þay smeten in-to merþe,
  639. Þat al watȝ blis & bonchef, þat breke hem bi-twene,
  640. & wynne,
  641. Þay lanced wordes gode,
  642. Much wele þen watȝ þer-inne,
  643. Gret perile bi-twene hem stod,
  644. Nif mare of hir knyȝt mynne.

  645. For þat prynce of pris de-presed hym so þikke.
  646. Nurned hym so neȝe þe þred, þat nede hym bi-houed,
  647. Oþer lach þer hir luf, oþer lodly re-fuse;
  648. He cared for his cortaysye, lest craþayn he were,
  649. & more for his meschef, ȝif he schulde make synne,
  650. & be traytor to þat tolke, þat þat telde aȝt.
  651. « God schylde, » quod þe schalk, « þat schal not be-falle! »
  652. With luf-laȝyng at lyt, he layd hym by-syde
  653. Alle þe specheȝ of specialté þat sprange of her mouthe.
  654. Quod þat burde to þe burne, « Blame ȝe disserue,
  655. « Ȝif ȝe luf not þat lyf þat ȝe lye nexte,
  656. « Bifore alle þe wyȝeȝe in þe worlde, wounded in hert,
  657. « Bot if ȝe haf a lemman, a leuer, þat yow lykeȝ better,
  658. « & folden fayth to þat fre, festned so harde,
  659. « Þat yow lausen ne lyst, & þat I leue nouþe;
  660. « & þat ȝe telle me þat, now trwly I pray yow,
  661. « For alle þe lufeȝ vpon lyue, layne not þe soþe,
  662. for gile. »
  663. Þe knyȝt sayde, « Be sayn Ion, »
  664. & smeþely con he smyle,
  665. « In fayth I welde riȝt non,
  666. « Ne non wil welde þe quile. »

  667. « Þat is a worde, » quod þat wyȝt, « þat worst is of alle,
  668. « Bot I am swared for soþe, þat sore me þinkkeȝ;
  669. « Kysse me now coraly, & I schal cach heþen,
  670. « I may bot mourne vpon molde, as may þat much louyes. »
  671. Sykande ho sweȝe doun, & semly hym kyssed,
  672. & siþen ho seueres hym fro, & says as ho stondes,
  673. « Now, dere, at þis de-partyng, do me þis ese,
  674. « Gif me sumquat of þy gifte, þi gloue if hit were,
  675. « Þat I may mynne on þe mon, my mournyng to lassen. »
  676. « Now Iwysse, » quod þat wyȝe, « I wolde I hade here
  677. « Þe leuest þing for þy luf, þat I in londe welde,
  678. « For ȝe haf deserued, forsoþe, sellyly ofte
  679. « More rewarde bi resoun, þen I reche myȝt,
  680. « Bot to del yow for drurye, þat dawed bot neked;
  681. « Hit is not your honour to haf at þis tyme
  682. « A gloue for a garysoun, of Gawayneȝgifteȝ,
  683. « & I am here [on] an erande in erdeȝ vncouþe,
  684. « & haue no men wyth no maleȝ, with menskful þingeȝ;
  685. « Þat mislykeȝ me ladé, for luf at þis tyme,
  686. « Iche tolke mon do as he is tan, tas to non ille,
  687. ne pine. »
  688. « Nay, hende of hyȝe honours, »
  689. Qyod þat lufsum vnder lyne,
  690. « Þaȝ I hade oȝt of youreȝ,
  691. « Ȝet schulde ȝe haue of myne. »

  692. Ho raȝt hym a riche rynk of red golde werkeȝ,
  693. Wyth a starande ston, stondande alofte,
  694. Þat bere blusschande bemeȝ as þe bryȝt sunne;
  695. Wyt ȝe wel, hit watȝ worth wele ful hoge.
  696. Bot þe renk hit renayed, & redyly he sayde,
  697. « I wil no gifteȝ for doge, my gay, at þis tyme;
  698. « I haf none tow to norne, ne noȝt wyl I take. »
  699. Ho bede hit hym ful bysily, & he hier bode wernes,
  700. & swere swyftel[y] his sothe, þat he hit sese nolde;
  701. & ho sore þat he forsoke, & sayde þer-after,
  702. « If ȝe renay my rynk, to ryche for hit semeȝ,
  703. « Ȝe wolde not so hyȝly halden be to me,
  704. « I schal gif yow my girdel, þat gaynes yow lasse. »
  705. Ho laȝt a lace lyȝtly, þat lekevmbe hir sydeȝ,
  706. Knit vpon hir kyrtel, vnder þe clere mantyle,
  707. Gered hit watȝ with grene sylke, & with golde schaped,
  708. Noȝt bot arounde brayden, beten with fyngres;
  709. & þat ho bede to þe burne, & blyþely bi-soȝt
  710. Þaȝ hit vn-worþi were, þat he hit take wolde.
  711. & he nay þat he nolde neghe in no wyse,
  712. Nauþer golde ne garysoun, er God hym grace sende,
  713. To acheue to þe chaunce þat he hade chosen þere.
  714. « & þerefore, I pray yow, displese yow noȝt,
  715. « & letteȝ be your bisinesse, for I bayþe hit yow neuer
  716. to graunte;
  717. « I am derely to yow biholde,
  718. « Bi-cause of your sembelaunt,
  719. « & euer in hot & colde
  720. « To be your trwe seruaunt. »

  721. « Now forsake ȝe þis silke, » sayde þe burde þenne,
  722. « For hit is symple in hit-self, & so hit wel semeȝ?
  723. « Lo! so hit is littel, & lasse hit is worþy;
  724. « Bot who-so knew þe costes þat knit ar þer-inne,
  725. « He wolde hit prayse at more prys, parauenture;
  726. « For quat gome so is gorde with þis grene lace,
  727. « While he hit hade hemely halched aboute,
  728. « Þer is no haþel vnder heuen to-hewe hym þat myȝt;
  729. « For he myȝt not he slayn, for slayȝt vpon erþe. »
  730. Þen kest þe knyȝt, & hit come to his hert,
  731. Hit were a Iuel for þe Iopardé, þat hym iugged were,
  732. When he acheued to þe chapel, his chek forto fech;
  733. Myȝ he haf slypped to þe vn-slayn, þe sleȝt were noble.
  734. Þenne ho þulged with hir þrepe, & þoled hir to speke,
  735. & ho bere on hym þe belt, & bede hit hym swyþe,
  736. & he granted, & [ho] hym gafe with a goud wylle,
  737. & bisoȝt hym, for hir sake, disceuer hit neuer,
  738. Bot to lelly layne for hir lorde; þe leude hym acordeȝ.
  739. Þat neuer wyȝe schulde hit wyt, Iwysse, bot þay twayne,
  740. for noȝte;
  741. He þonkked hir oft ful swyþe,
  742. Ful þro with hert & þoȝt.
  743. Bi þat on þrynne syþe,
  744. He hatȝ kyst þe knyȝt so toȝt.

  745. Thenne lachcheȝ ho hir leue, & leueȝ hym þere,
  746. for more myrþe of þat mon moȝt ho not gete;
  747. When ho watȝ gon, sir G. gereȝ hym sone,
  748. Rises, & riches hym in araye noble,
  749. Lays vp þe luf-lace, þe lady hym raȝt,
  750. Hid hit ful holdely, þer he hit eft fonde;
  751. Syþen cheuely to þe chapel choses he þe waye,
  752. Preuely aproched to a prest, & prayed hym þere
  753. Þat he wolde lyfte his lyf, & lern hym better,
  754. How his sawle schulde be saued, when he schuld seye heþen.
  755. Þere he schorf hym schyrly, & schewed his mysdedeȝ,
  756. Of þe more & þe mynne, & merci besecheȝ,
  757. & of absolucioun he on þe segge calles;
  758. & he asoyled hym surely, & sette hym so clene,
  759. As domeȝ-day schulde haf ben diȝt on þe morn.
  760. & syþen he mace hym as mery among þe fre ladyes,
  761. With comlych caroles, & alle kynnes ioye,
  762. As neuer he did bot þat daye, to þe derk nyȝt,
  763. with blys;
  764. Vche mon hade daynte þare,
  765. Of hym, & sayde Iwysse,
  766. Þus myry he watȝ neuer are,
  767. Syn he com hider, er þis.

  768. Now hym lenge is þat lee, þer luf hym bi-tyde;
  769. Ȝet is þe lorde on þe launde, ledande his gomnes,
  770. He hatȝ for faren þis fox, þat he folȝed longe;
  771. As he sprent ouer a spenné, to spye þe schrewe,
  772. Þer as he herd þe howndes, þat hasted hym swyþe,
  773. Renaud com richchande þurȝ a roȝe greue,
  774. & alle þe rabel in a res, ryȝt at his heleȝ.
  775. Þe wyȝe watȝ war of þe wylde, & warly abides,
  776. & braydeȝ out þe bryȝt bronde, & at þe best casteȝ;
  777. & he schunt for þe scharp, & schulde haf arered,
  778. A rach rapes hym to, ryȝt er he myȝt,
  779. & ryȝt bifore þe hors fete þay fel on hym alle,
  780. & woried me þis wyly wyth a wroth noyse.
  781. Þe lorde lyȝteȝ bilyue, & cacheȝ by sone,
  782. Rased hym ful radly out of þe rach mouþes,
  783. Haldeȝ heȝe ouer his hede, haloweȝ faste,
  784. & þer bayen hym mony bray houndeȝ;
  785. Huntes hyȝed hem þeder, with horneȝ ful mony,
  786. Ay re-chatande aryȝt til þay þe renk seȝen;
  787. Bi þat watȝ comen his compenyny noble,
  788. Alle þat euer ber bugle blowed at ones,
  789. & alle þise oþer halowed, þat hade no hornes,
  790. Hit watȝ þe myriest mute þat euer men herde,
  791. Þe rich rurd þat þer watȝ raysed for renaude saule,
  792. with lote;
  793. Hor houndeȝ þay þer rewarde,
  794. Her hedeȝ þay fawne & frote,
  795. & syþen þay tan raynarde,
  796. & tyrnen of his cote.

  797. & þenne þay helden to home, for hit watȝ nieȝ nyȝt,
  798. Strakande ful stoutly in hor store horneȝ;
  799. Þe lorde is lyȝt at þe laste at hys lef home,
  800. Fyndeȝ fire vpon flet, þe freke þer by-side,
  801. Sir Gawayn þe gode, þat glad watȝ with alle,
  802. Among þe ladies for luf he ladde much ioye,
  803. He were a bleaunt of blwe, þat bradde to þe erþe,
  804. His surkot semed hym wel, þat softe watȝ forred,
  805. & his hode of þat ilke henged on his schulder,
  806. Blande al of blaunner were boþe al aboute.
  807. He meteȝ me þis god mon in myddeȝ þe flore,
  808. & al with gomen he hym gret, & goudly he sayde,
  809. « I schal fylle vpon fyrst oure forwardeȝ nouþe,
  810. « Þat we spedly han spoken, þer spared watȝ no drynk; »
  811. Þen acolas he [þe] knyȝt, & kysses hym þryes,
  812. As sauerly & sadly as he hem sette couþe.
  813. « Bi Kryst, » quod þat oþer knyȝt, « ȝe cach much sele,
  814. « In cheuisaunce of þis chaffer, ȝif ȝe hade goud chepeȝ. »
  815. « Ȝe of þe chepe no charg, » quod chefly þat oþer,
  816. « As is pertly payed þe chepeȝ þat I aȝte. »
  817. « Mary, » quod þat oþer mon, « myn is bi-hynde,
  818. « For I haf hunted al þis day, & noȝt haf I geten,
  819. « Bot þis foule fox felle, þe fende haf þe godeȝ,
  820. « & þat is ful pore, for to pay for suche prys þinges,
  821. « As ȝe haf þryȝt me here, þro suche þre cosses,
  822. so gode. »
  823. « I-noȝ, » quod sir Gawayn,
  824. « I þonk yow, bi þe rode; »
  825. & how þe fox watȝ slayn,
  826. He tolde hym, as þay stode.

  827. With merþe & mynstralsye, wyth meteȝ at hor wylle,
  828. Þay maden as mery as any men moȝten,
  829. With laȝyng of ladies, with loteȝ of bordes;
  830. Gawayn & þe gode mon so glad were þay boþe,
  831. Bot if þe douthe had doted, oþer dronken ben oþer,
  832. Boþe þe mon & þe meyny maden mony iapeȝ,
  833. Til þe sesoun watȝ seȝen, þat þay seuer moste;
  834. Burneȝ to hor bedde be-houed at þe laste.
  835. Þenne loȝly his leue at þe lorde fyrst
  836. Fochcheȝ þis fre mon, & fayre he hym þonkkeȝ;
  837. « Of such a sellyly soiorne, as I haf hade here,
  838. « Your honour, at þis hyȝe fest, þe hyȝe kyng yow ȝelde!
  839. « I ȝef yow me for on of youreȝ, if youwre-self lykeȝ,
  840. « For I mot nedes, as ȝe wot, meue to morne;
  841. « & ȝe me take sum tolke, to teche, as ȝe hyȝt,
  842. « Þe gate to þe grene chapel, as god wyl me suffer
  843. « To dele, on nwȝereȝ day, þe dome of my wyrdes. »
  844. « In god fayþe, » quod þe god mon, « wyth a goud wylle;
  845. « Al þat euer I yow hyȝt, halde schal I rede. »
  846. Þer asynges he a seruaunt, to sett hym in þe waye,
  847. & coundue hym by þe downeȝ, þat he no drechch had,
  848. For to f[e]rk þurȝ þe fryth, & fare at þe gaynest,
  849. bi greue.
  850. Þe lorde Gawayn con þonk,
  851. Such worchip he wolde hym weue;
  852. Þen at þo ladyeȝ wlonk.
  853. Þe knyȝt hatȝ tan his leue.

  854. With care & wyth kyssyng he carppeȝ hem tille,
  855. & fele þryuande þonkkeȝ he þrat hom to haue,
  856. & þay ȝelden hym aȝay[n] ȝeply þat ilk;
  857. Þay bikende hym to Kryst, with ful colde sykyngeȝ.
  858. Syþen fro þe meyny he menskly de-partes;
  859. Vche mon þat he mette, he made hem a þonke,
  860. For his seruyse, & his solace, & his sere pyne,
  861. Þat þay wyth busynes had ben, aboute hym to serue;
  862. & vche segge as sore, to seuer with hym þere,
  863. As þay hade wonde worþyly with þat wlonk euer.
  864. Þen with ledes & lyȝt he watȝ ladde to his chambre,
  865. & blybely broȝt to his bedde, to be at his rest;
  866. Ȝif he ne slepe soundyly, say ne dar I,
  867. For he hade muche on þe morn to mynne, ȝif he wolde,
  868. in þoȝt;
  869. Let hym lyȝe þere stille,

FYTTE THE FOURTH.

  1. Now neȝeȝ þe nwȝere, & þe nyȝt passeȝ,
  2. Þe day dryueȝ to þe derk, as dryȝtyn biddeȝ;
  3. Bot wylde wedereȝ of þe worlde wakned þeroute,
  4. Clowdes kesten kenly þe colde to þe erþe,

  7. Þe werbeland wynde wapped fro þe hyȝe,
  8. & drof vche dale ful of dryftes ful grete.
  9. Þe leude lystened ful wel, þat leȝ in his bedde,
  10. Þaȝ he lowkeȝ his liddeȝ, ful lyttel he slepes;
  11. Bi vch kok þat crue, he knwe wel þe steuen.
  12. De-liuerly he dressed vp, er þe day sprenged,
  13. For þere watȝ lyȝt of a lau[m[pe, þat lemed in his chambre;
  14. He called to his chamberlayn, þat cofly hym swared,
  15. & bede hym bryng hym his bruny, & his blonk sadel;
  16. Þat oþer ferkeȝ hym vp, & fecheȝ hym his wedeȝ,
  17. & grayþeȝ me sir Gawayn vpon a grett wyse.
  18. Fyrst he clad hym in his cloþeȝ, þe colde for to were;
  19. & syþen his oþer harnays, þat holdely watȝ keped,
  20. Boþe his paunce, & his plateȝ, piked ful clene,

  23. to þonk;
  24. He hade vpon vche pece,

  26. Þe gayest in to Grece,

  39. Vpon þat ryol red cloþe, þat ryche watȝ to schewe.
  40. Bot wered not þis ilk wyȝe for wele þis gordel,
  41. For pryde of þe pendaunteȝ, þaȝ polyst þay were,
  42. & þaȝ þe glyterande golde glent vpon endeȝ,
  43. Bot forto sauen hym-self, when suffer hym by-houed,
  44. To byde bale with-oute debate, of bronde hym to were,
  45. oþer knyffe;
  46. Bi þat þe bolde mon boun,
  47. Wynneȝ þeroute bilyue,
  48. Alle þe meyny of renoun,
  49. He þonkkeȝ ofte ful ryue.

  56. « Þe mon hem maynteines, ioy mot þay haue;
  57. « Þe leue lady, on lyue luf hir bityde;
  58. « Ȝif þay for charyté cherysen a gest,
  59. « & halden honour in her honde, þe haþel hem ȝelde,
  60. « Þat haldeȝ þe heuen vpon hyȝe, & also yow alle!
  61. « & ȝif I myȝt lyf vpon londe lede any quyle,

  65. Gordeȝ to Gryngolet, with his gilt heleȝ,

  68. His haþel on hors watȝ þenne,

  70. « Þis kastel to Kryst I kenne,
  71. « He gef hit ay god chaunce! «

  74. Þe burne blessed hym bilyue, & þe bredeȝ passed;
  75. Prayses þe porter, bifore þe prynce kneled,
  76. Gef hym God & goud day, þat Gawayn he saue;

  78. Þat schulde teche hym to tourne to þat tene place,

  80. Þay boȝen bi bonkkeȝ, þer boȝeȝ ar bare,
  81. Þay clombed bi clyffeȝ, þer clengeȝ þe colde;
  82. Þe heuen watȝvp halt, bot vgly þer vnder,
  83. Mist muged on þe mor, malt on þe mounteȝ,
  84. Vch hille hade a hatte, a myst-hakel huge;
  85. Brokeȝ byled, & breke, bi bonkkeȝ aboute,
  86. Schyre schateraunde on schoreȝ, þer þay doun schowued.
  87. Welawylle watȝ þe way, þer þay bi wod schulden,
  88. Til hit watȝ sone sesoun, þat þe sunne ryses,
  89. þat tyde;
  90. Þay were on a hille ful hyȝe,

  92. Þe burne þat rod hym by
  93. Bede his mayster abide.

  316. Þe scharp schrank to þe flesche þurȝ þe schyre grece,
  317. Þat þe schene blod over his schulderes schot to þe erþe.
  318. & quen þe burne seȝ þe blode blenk on þe snawe,
  319. He sprit forth spenne fote more þen a spere lenþe,
  320. Hent heterly his helme, & on his hed cast,
  321. Schot with his schuldereȝ his fayre schelde vnder,
  322. Braydeȝ out a bryȝt sworde, & bremely he spekeȝ;
  323. Neuer syn þat he watȝ burne borne of his moder,
  324. Watȝ he neuer in þis worlde, wyȝe half so blyþe: —
  325. « Blynne, burne, of þy bur, bede me no mo;
  326. « I haf a stroke in þis sted with-oute stryf hent,
  327. « & if þow recheȝ me any mo, I redyly schal quyte,
  328. « & ȝelde ȝederly aȝayn, & þer to ȝe tryst,
  329. & foo;
  330. « Bot on stroke here me falleȝ,
  331. « Þe couenaunt schop ryȝt so,
  332. « [Sikered] in Arþureȝ halleȝ,
  333. « & þer-fore, hende, now hoo! »

  334. The haþel heldet hym fro, & on his ax rested,
  335. Sette þe schaft vpon schore, & to be scharp lened,
  336. & loked to þe leude, þat on þe launde ȝede,
  337. How þat doȝty dredles deruely þer stondeȝ,
  338. Armed ful aȝleȝ; in hert hit hym lykeȝ.
  339. Þenn he meleȝ muryly, wyth a much steuen,
  340. & wyth a r[a]ykande rurde he to þe renk sayde,
  341. « Bolde burne, on þis bent be not so gryndel;
  342. « No mon here vn-manerly þe myst-boden habbe,
  343. « Ne kyd, bot as couenaunde, at kyngeȝ kort schaped;
  344. « I hayȝt þe a strok, & þou hit hatȝ, halde þe wel payed,
  345. « I relece þe of þe remnaunt, of ryȝtes alle oþer;
  346. « Ȝif I deliuer had bene, a boffet, paraunter,
  347. « I couþe wroþeloker haf waret, [&] to þe haf wroȝt anger.
  348. « Fyrst I mansed þe muryly, with a mynt one,
  349. « & roue þe wyth no rof, sore with ryȝt I þe profered,
  350. « For þe forwarde that we fest in þe fyrst nyȝt,
  351. « & þou trystyly þe trawþe & trwly me haldeȝ,
  352. « Al þe gayne þow me gef, as god mon shulde;
  353. « Þat oþer munt for þe morne, mon, I þe profered,
  354. « Þou kyssedes my clere wyf, þe cosseȝ me raȝteȝ,
  355. « For boþe two here I þe bede bot two bare myntes,
  356. boute scaþe;
  357. « Trwe mon trwe restore,
  358. « Þenne þar mon drede no waþe;
  359. « At þe þrid þou fayled þore,
  360. « & þer-for þat tappe ta þe.

  361. « For hit is my wede þat þou wereȝ, þat ilke wouen girdel,
  362. « Myn owen wyf hit þe weued, I wot wel forsoþe;
  363. « Now know I wel þy cosses, & þy costes als,
  364. « & þe wowyng of my wyf, I wroȝt hit myseluen;
  365. « I sende hir to asay þe, & sothly me þynkkeȝ,
  366. « On þe fautlest freke, þat euer on fote ȝede;
  367. « As perle bi þe quite pese is of prys more,
  368. « So is Gawayn, in god fayth, bi oþer gay knyȝteȝ.
  369. « Bot here yow lakked a lyttel, sir, & lewte yow wonted,
  370. « Bot þat watȝ for no wylyde werke, ne wowyng nauþer,
  371. « Bot for ȝe lufed your lyf, þe lasse I yow blame. »
  372. Þat oþer stif mon in study stod a gret whyle;
  373. So agreued for greme he gryed with-inne,
  374. Alle þe blode of his brest blende in his face,
  375. Þat al he schrank for schome, þat þe schalk talked.
  376. Þe forme worde vpon folde, þat þe freke meled, —
  377. « Corsed worth cowarddyse & couetyse boþe!
  378. « In yow is vylany & vyse, þat vertue disstryeȝ. »
  379. Þenne he kaȝt to þe knot, & þe kest lawseȝ,
  380. Brayde broþely þe belt to þe burne seluen:
  381. « Lo! þer þe falssyng, foule mot hit falle!
  382. « For care of þy knokke cowardyse me taȝt
  383. « To a-corde me with couetyse, my kynde to for-sake,
  384. « Þat is larges & lewte, þat longeȝ to knyȝteȝ.
  385. « Now am I fawty, & falce, & ferde haf ben euer,
  386. « Of trecherye & vn-trawþe boþe bityde sorȝe
  387. & care!
  388. « I bi-knowe yow, knyȝt, here stylle,
  389. « Al fawty is my fare,
  390. « Leteȝ me ouer-take your wylle,
  391. « & efle I schal be ware. »

  392. Thenne loȝe þat oþer leude, & luflyly sayde,
  393. « I halde hit hardily hole, þe harme þat I hade;
  394. « Þou art confessed so clene, be-knowen of þy mysses,
  395. « & hatȝ þe penaunce apert, of þe poynt of myn egge,
  396. « I halde þe polysed of þat plyȝt, & pured as clene,
  397. « As þou hadeȝ neuer forfeted, syþen þou watȝ fyrst borne.
  398. « & I gif þe, sir, þe gurdel þat is golde hemmed;
  399. « For hit is grene as my goune, sir G:, ȝe maye
  400. « Þenk vpon þis ilke þrepe, þer þou forth þryngeȝ
  401. « Among prynces of prys, & þis a pure token
  402. « Of þe chaunce of þe grene chapel, at cheualrous knyȝteȝ;
  403. « & ȝe schal in þis nwe ȝer aȝayn to my woneȝ,
  404. « & we schyn reuel þe remnaunt of þis ryche fest,
  405. ful bene. »
  406. Þer laþed hym fast þe lorde,
  407. & sayde, « With my wyf, I wene,
  408. « We schal yow wel acorde,
  409. « Þat watȝ your enmy kene. »

  410. « Nay, for soþe, » quod þe segge, & sesed hys helme,
  411. & hatȝ hit of hendely, & þe haþel þonkkeȝ,
  412. « I haf soiorned sadly, sele yow bytyde,
  413. « & he ȝelde hit yow ȝare, þat ȝarkkeȝ al menskes!
  414. « & comaundeȝ me to þat cortays, your comlych fere,
  415. « Boþe þat on & þat oþer, myn honoured ladyȝ.
  416. « Þat þus hor knyȝt wyth hor kest han koyntly bigyled.
  417. « Bot hit is no ferly, þaȝ a fole madde,
  418. « & þurȝ wyles of wymmen be wonen to sorȝe;
  419. « For so watȝ Adam in erde with one bygyled,
  420. « & Salamon with fele sere, & Samson eft soneȝ,
  421. « Dalyda dalt hym hys wyrde, & Dauyth þer-after
  422. « Watȝ blended with Barsabe, þat much bale þoled.
  423. « Now þese were wrathed wyth her wyles, hit were a wynne huge,
  424. « To luf hom wel, & leue hem not, a leude þat couþe,
  425. « For þes wer forne þe freest þat folȝed alle þe sele,
  426. « Ex-elently of alle þys oþer, vnder heuen-ryche,
  427. þat mused;
  428. « & alle þay were bi-wyled,
  429. « With wymmen þat þay vsed,
  430. « Þaȝ I be now bigyled,
  431. « Me þink me burde be excused. »

  432. « Bot your gordel, » quod G: « God yow for-ȝelde!
  433. « Þat wyl I welde wyth good wylle, not for þe wynne golde,
  434. « Ne þe saynt, ne þe sylk, ne þe syde pendaundes,
  435. « For wele, ne for worchyp, ne for þe wlonk werkkeȝ,
  436. « Bot in syngne of my surfet I schal se hit ofte;
  437. « When I ride in renoun, remorde to myseluen
  438. « Þe faut & þe fayntyse of þe flesche crabbed,
  439. « How tender hit is to entyse teches of fylþe;
  440. « & þus, quen pryde schal me pryk, for prowes of armes,
  441. « Þe loke to þis luf lace schal leþe my hert.
  442. « Bot on I wolde yow pray, displeses yow neuer;
  443. « Syn ȝe be lorde of þe ȝonde[r] londe, þer I haf lent inne,
  444. « Wyth yow wyth worschyp, — þe wyȝe hit yow ȝelde
  445. « Þat vp-haldeȝ þe heuen, & on hyȝ sitteȝ, —
  446. « How norne ȝe yowre ryȝt nome, & þenne no more? »
  447. « Þat schal I telle þe trwly, » quod þat oþer þenne,
  448. « Bernlak de Hautdesert I hat in þis londe,
  449. « Þurȝ myȝt of Morgane la Faye, þat in my hous lenges,
  450. « & koyntyse of clergye, bi craftes wel lerned,
  451. « Þe maystres of Merlyn, mony ho taken;
  452. « For ho hatȝ dalt drwry ful dere sum tyme,
  453. « With þat conable klerk, þat knowes alle your knyȝteȝ
  454. at hame;
  455. « Morgne þe goddes,
  456. « Þer-fore hit is hir name;
  457. « Weldeȝ non so hyȝe hawtesse,
  458. « Þat ho ne con make ful tame.

  459. « Ho wayned me vpon þis wyse to your wynne halle,
  460. « For to assay þe surquidre, ȝif hit soth were,
  461. « Þat rennes of þe grete renoun of þe Rounde Table;
  462. « Ho wayned me þis wonder, your wytteȝ to reue,
  463. « For to haf greued Gaynour, & gart hir to dyȝe.
  464. « With gopnyng of þat ilke gomen, þat gostlych speked,
  465. « With his hede in his honde, bifore þe hyȝe table.
  466. « Þat is ho þat is at home, þe auncian lady;

  468. « Þe duches doȝter of Tyntagelle, þat dere Vter after
  469. « Hade Arþur vpon, þat aþel is nowþe

  471. « Make myry in my hous, my meny þe louies,
  472. « & I wol þe as wel, wyȝe, bi my faythe,
  473. « As any gome vnder God, for þy grete trauþe. »

  475. Þay acolen & kyssen, [bikennen] ayþer
  476. To þe prynce of paradise, & parten ryȝt þere,
  477. on coolde;
  478. Gawayn on blonk ful bene,
  479. To þe kyngeȝ burȝ buskeȝ bolde,
  480. & þe knyȝt in þe enker grene,

  482. Wylde wayeȝ in þe worlde Wowen now rydeȝ,

  484. Ofte he herbered in house, & ofte al þeroute,

  490. Loken vnder his lyfte arme, þe lace, with a knot,
  491. In tokenyng he watȝ tane in tech of a faute;
  492. & þus he commes to þe court, knyȝt al in sounde.
  493. Þer wakned wele in þat wone, when wyst þe grete,
  494. Þat gode G: watȝ commen, gayn hit hym þoȝt;

  499. Þe chaunce of þe chapel, þe chere of þe knyȝt,
  500. Þe luf of þe ladi, þe lace at þe last.

  504. He tened quen he schulde telle,

  506. Þe blod in his face con melle,
  507. When he hit schulde schewe, for schame.

  511. « Of couardise & couetyse, þat I haf caȝt þare,
  512. « Þis is þe token of vn-trawþe, þat I am tan inne,
  513. « & I mot nedeȝ hit were, wyle I may last;
  514. « For mon may hyden his harme, bot vnhap ne may hit,
  515. « For þer hit oneȝ is tachched, twynne wil hit neuer. »

  517. Laȝen loude þer-at, & luflyly acorden,
  518. Þat lordes & ladis, þat longed to þe Table,
  519. Vche burne of þe broþer-hede a bauderyk schulde haue,
  520. A bende, a belef hym aboute, of a bryȝt grene,
  521. & þat, for sake of þat segge, in swete to were.

  529. I-wysse;
